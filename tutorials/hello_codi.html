Hello Codi Tutorial

1. Introduction

Learn the basics of sound and touch in a mobile app by getting Codi to buzz when you tap on him!

If you have a project of your own, skip to step five to see how a button can make a noise when you tap on it

Connect using the companion app to test your work on your mobile device. If you don't know how to connect, click here.

2. Add the Bee

Codi will be a button because we want to be able to click on him in our app.

Find the button component in the User Interface section of your Pallete. Drag the button component onto your Viewer. A new button will now appear on the Viewer.

Check to see if you can see your button in your companion app.

Now we will make the button look like Codi. Find the image property of the button, click on it and then select the Codi.png file

You will see that your button is now the image of Codi, but it has text on it! Fix this by deleting the text for the button blank under the button properties.

3. Add a Label

Add a label to your app so that your friends will know that they should tap the bee.

Drag and drop a label component onto your viewer

Under properties for the label, change the text to say "touch the bee"

You can change other properties like TextColor or TextSize. Your app should look something like this now:

4. Add Sound

Now we will add a sound component to the app. You can find it under the media components. Drag and drop the sound component onto the viewer.

The component will appear below the screen in your "Non-visible components".

Under properties, change the source of your sound. Select the Bee_Sound.mp3 and click OK

5. Play the Sound

Tell Codi when he should make a buzzing noise by going into the blocks editor.

Go to the Button1 blocks which are all blocks specific to the functionality of the button

Drag and drop the when Button1.Click block into your viewer. This block will tell the button to do "something" when it is clicked. For us the "something" will be making a noise.

Now go to the sound1 blocks.

Find the block that is sound.play and drag it onto your viewer.

Now nest the two blocks so that Codi plays a sound when he is clicked.

6. Test your app!

Does Codi buzz when you tap him?
